## Discussion Points

### 1. Copy Style

**Current approach:**
- Mix of casual and formal Indonesian
- Some inconsistent tone across pages

**Proposed approach:**
- **Headlines:** English (impactful, modern)
- **Body copy:** Professional Indonesian (sopan tapi tidak kaku)
- **CTAs:** Short, action-oriented Indonesian

**Examples:**

| Element | Current | Proposed |
|---------|---------|----------|
| Hero headline | "No System, No Growth" | Keep (English, impactful) |
| Hero subtext | Casual Indonesian | Professional but warm |
| CTA button | "Dapatkan Sistemnya" | Shorter: "Mulai Sekarang" or keep? |
| Feature descriptions | Mixed | Consistent professional tone |

### 3. Professional Indonesian Guidelines

**Do:**
- Use "Anda" (formal) or "kamu" (friendly) — pick one
- Clear, concise sentences
- Avoid slang in main copy
- Active voice

**Don't:**
- Mix formal/informal in same section
- Overuse English words when Indonesian works
- Use corporate jargon

### 4. English Headlines Strategy

Where to use English:
- Main hero headline (impactful)
- Section titles (if short and punchy)
- Feature names (if product-related)

Where to keep Indonesian:
- Body copy and descriptions
- CTAs and buttons
- Form labels
- Error messages

---

## Decisions Made

1. **Formality level:** "Anda" (professional) ✓
2. **Headlines:** English headlines, Indonesian body ✓
3. **CTA:** Keep "Dapatkan Sistemnya" ✓
4. **Priority:** Landing → Pricing → CRM ✓
